Irene applied for a job through an international recruitment agency that connected household workers with families in the U.S. She accepted a job with a California couple who promised they would pay her $1,500 a month and sponsor her temporary work visa. Upon her arrival, they forbade Irene from ever leaving the home, always arming the security system to keep her confined. For 18 hours a day, Irene cooked, cleaned, and cared for the children, but was never paid.

Jasmine was 18 years old when she ran away from her home in Missouri with her 19-year-old boyfriend Steven. 'Now that we’re going to live together, you’ll need to earn some money,' Steven told her. And then he drove her to a truck stop on a local interstate. Every night, Jasmine was taken to a different truck stop and forced to go door to door offering commercial sex.

Fifteen-year-old Marisol had been living in Texas for eight months, working as a waitress and forced to sell sex at a cantina. She had come from Mexico, promised a job that would earn her enough money to support her family back home. She was smuggled across the border by an organized network and housed in a cramped apartment with ten other girls and women.

Over the course of 2002 to 2018, over 50,000 people were reported as a victim of human trafficking.

Human trafficking is a modern-day slavery in which victims are exploited through the use of violence, deception, or coersion for the purpose of sex, organ removal, forced marriage, military, or involuntary labor.

Victims of human trafficking ranges from young children to grown up men and women, U.S. citizens to any lawful permanent residents of foreign nations, educated to non-educated, and anyone who lives in urban, suburban, and rural areas. In other words, ANYONE can become a victims of human trafficking.

Human trafficking involves recruitment, harbouring or transporting people into a situation of exploitation through the use of violence, deception or coercion.

Traffickers use various means of control to exploit the victims. Some of these includes: Psychological Abuse, Threats, False Promises, Debt Bondages, Use of Children, and more. Traffickers utilize specific means of control that is most suitable for the targeted victims. We observed how type of exploitation changes the means of control.

The most prominent means of control for Sexual Exploitation was "Psychological Abuse". Followed by Restricts Movement, Physical Abuse, and Threats.

As you can see in the bar chart, victims of sexual exploitation are mostly recruited through use of violence. They are abused psychologically and physically, and were forced to provide commercial sex.

Sex trafficking has devastating consequences for the trafficked individual. Victims may suffer from long-lasting physical and psychological trauma, disease (HIV/AIDS), drug addiction, malnutrition and social ostracism.

On the other hand, most prominent means of control for Labour Exploitation was "False Promises". Followed by Takes Earnings, Excessive Working Hours, and Psychological Abuse.

Unlike the sexual exploitation, victims of labour exploitation is mostly recruited by deception or coercion.

Most victims of the labour explitation are promised a job and an income, but ends up getting locked up and forced to work.

People can be trafficked for many different forms of exploitation such as forced prostitution, forced labour, forced begging, forced criminality, domestic servitude, forced marraige, and forced organ removal.
