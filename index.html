Human trafficking is a modern-day slavery in which victims are exploited through the use of violence, deception, or coersion for the purpose of sex, organ removal, forced marriage, military, or involuntary labor.

Victims of human trafficking ranges from young children to grown up men and women, U.S. citizens to any lawful permanent residents of foreign nations, educated to non-educated, and anyone who lives in urban, suburban, and rural areas. In other words, ANYONE can become a victims of human trafficking.

Human trafficking involves recruitment, harbouring or transporting people into a situation of exploitation through the use of violence, deception or coercion.

Traffickers use various means of control to exploit the victims. Some of these includes: Psychological Abuse, Threats, False Promises, Debt Bondages, Use of Children, and more. Traffickers utilize specific means of control that is most suitable for the targeted victims. We observed how type of exploitation changes the means of control, and saw a clear differences.

The most popular means of control for Sexual Exploitation was "Psychological Abuse". Followed by Restricts Movement, Physical Abuse, and Threats.

As you can see in the bar chart, victims of sexual exploitation are mostly recruited through use of violence. They are abused psychologically and physically, and were forced to provide commercial sex.

Sex trafficking has devastating consequences for the trafficked individual. Victims may suffer from long-lasting physical and psychological trauma, disease (HIV/AIDS), drug addiction, malnutrition and social ostracism.

On the other hand, most popular means of control for Labour Exploitation was "False Promises". Followed by Takes Earnings, Excessive Working Hours, and Psychological Abuse.

Unlike the sexual exploitation, victims of labour exploitation is mostly recruited by deception or coercion.

Most victims of the labour explitation are promised a job and an income, but ends up getting locked up and forced to work.

People can be trafficked for many different forms of exploitation such as forced prostitution, forced labour, forced begging, forced criminality, domestic servitude, forced marraige, and forced organ removal.
